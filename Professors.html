Albus Dumbledore
Albus Dumbledore was the wise and revered headmaster of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Renowned for his deep knowledge of magic and his involvement in pivotal historical events, Dumbledore was a symbol of kindness, courage, and wisdom. His towering figure often adorned with a long silver beard, he wore half-moon spectacles and had an air of mystery about him. Despite his gentle demeanor, Dumbledore was a formidable wizard, known for his leadership in the fight against dark forces, particularly his defeat of the dark wizard Gellert Grindelwald. His leadership, guidance, and unwavering belief in the power of love were central to the success of Harry Potter and his friends in their battles against Lord Voldemort.

Rubeus Hagrid
Rubeus Hagrid was the Keeper of Keys and Grounds at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, as well as the Care of Magical Creatures professor. A giant of a man with a heart even bigger, Hagrid was known for his love of magical creatures and his deep loyalty to his friends, especially Harry Potter. With his wild, untamed hair and bushy beard, Hagrid was often seen accompanied by his beloved creatures, such as the three-headed dog Fluffy and his dragon Norbert. Despite his rough appearance, Hagrid was a kind and trustworthy individual, always ready to lend a helping hand. His sense of adventure and strong sense of justice led him to become one of Harry’s most trusted allies in the fight against dark forces.

Severus Snape
Severus Snape was the Potions Master at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, as well as the Head of Slytherin House. A complex and enigmatic figure, Snape was known for his stern, often cruel demeanor and his dislike for Harry Potter. However, his true loyalties were revealed only in the end, as Snape's actions were motivated by a deep love for Lily Potter, Harry’s mother, and a commitment to protecting him. Despite his outwardly harsh persona, Snape was a skilled wizard with immense courage, who played a pivotal role in the defeat of Lord Voldemort. His dedication to his work as a spy and his tragic personal story made him one of the most misunderstood and complex characters in the wizarding world.

Minerva McGonagall
Minerva McGonagall was the Transfiguration professor at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry and Head of Gryffindor House. A strict but fair teacher, she was known for her no-nonsense approach to both teaching and discipline. McGonagall was also an animagus, able to transform into a cat at will. Her dedication to her students and her loyalty to the wizarding world was unwavering, as she played a crucial role in the fight against Lord Voldemort and in defending Hogwarts. Beneath her stern exterior, she cared deeply for the well-being of her students and was fiercely protective of them. McGonagall was also appointed Headmistress of Hogwarts following the battle with Voldemort.

Pomona Sprout
Pomona Sprout was the Head of Hufflepuff House and the Herbology professor at Hogwarts. Cheerful and knowledgeable, she was an expert in magical plants and played a vital role in defending the school during the Battle of Hogwarts by using magical flora like Mandrakes and Venomous Tentacula.

Remus Lupin
Remus Lupin was one of the most beloved Defense Against the Dark Arts professors at Hogwarts. He made lessons engaging and practical, teaching students to handle magical creatures and defend themselves. Despite his struggles as a werewolf, he earned respect for his wisdom, kindness, and exceptional teaching.

Filius Flitwick
Filius Flitwick was the Charms professor and Head of Ravenclaw House. Known for his small stature and cheerful demeanor, Flitwick was a skilled duelist and played a significant role in protecting Hogwarts during the Battle of Hogwarts, demonstrating exceptional mastery of spells.
